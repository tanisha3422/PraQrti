Psidium guajava

  • Scientific Name - Psidium guajava
  • Common Name - अमरूद
  • Family- Myrtaceae
  • Location- G20 lawn

Other Information:

  1. Flower colour: The flowers of Psidium guajava are typically white, with 4-5 petals and numerous stamens.
  2. Flowering stage: Guava trees typically bloom in the spring or early summer, depending on the climate and growing conditions.
  3. Fruit: Guava fruits are round or oval in shape and have a green or yellow skin when ripe. The flesh of the fruit is typically white, pink, or red, with numerous small seeds.
  4. Leaves: The leaves of Psidium guajava are typically glossy and dark green, with a distinctive oblong shape and prominent veins.
  5. Bark: The bark of guava trees is smooth and usually light brown or gray in color, with vertical striations.
  6. Growth habit: Guava trees have a dense, bushy growth habit and can reach heights of up to 10 meters if left unpruned.
  7. Medicinal properties: Various parts of the guava plant, including the leaves and fruit, have been used in traditional medicine for their antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antidiabetic properties.
